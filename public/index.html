Early islamic marriages
Traditions and politics

General information

Our story is about marriages behaviour of Quraysh tribe in early islamic period. And here we are trying to find some evidences of how marriage traditions might affect political system at that time or vice versa. Historically it's known that usually people in Arabian peninsula used to marry people from the same tribe, but it is not the constant. Below you can see what tribes Quraysh people used to marry with. This is overall graph of all generation we have in our database

Let's look at each generation separetely (graphs hoverable):

So, as we can see the number of Quraysh-to-Quraysh marriages remained almost the same throughout all 5 generations. However, marriages with other tribes fluactuated. Some of them appeared at some point of time, whereas others just vanished.

Basically, we are not going to explore every Qurayshi subtribe, but to focus on the most influential and major tribes who were the rulers of two biggest islamic caliphates (Umayyad Caliphate and Abbassid Caliphate): Umayyads and Hashimids
