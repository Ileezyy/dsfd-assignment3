Early islamic marriages
Traditions and politics

General information

Our story is about marriages behaviour of Quraysh tribe in early islamic period. And here we are trying to find some evidences of how marriage traditions might affect political system at that time or vice versa. Historically it's known that usually people in Arabian peninsula used to marry people from the same tribe, but it is not the constant. Below you can see what tribes Quraysh people used to marry with. So, as we can see the number of Quraysh-to-Quraysh marriages remained almost the same throughout all 5 generations. However, marriages with other tribes fluactuated. Some of them appeared at some point of time, whereas others just vanished.

Basically, we are not going to explore every Qurayshi subtribe, but to focus on the most influential and major tribes who were the rulers of two biggest islamic caliphates (Umayyad Caliphate and Abbassid Caliphate): Umayyads and Hashims

Below the family tree of these two clans:

Umayyads

Umayyads were a clan of the Quraysh tribe descended from Umayya ibn Abd Shams. The clan staunchly opposed the Islamic prophet Muhammad, but eventually embraced Islam before the latter's death in 632 (wikipedia)

They established Umayyad Caliphate. The first caliph was Muawiya ibn Abi Sufyan. The caliphate basically was ruled by two subclans: Marwanids and Sufyanids

Hashims

Hashims - descendants of Hashim ibn Abd Manaf. Common ancestor of Muhammad, Ali and Abbas. The term is also applied to the Prophet's family. Abbasids used the term to rally support for revolution.

Established the Abbasid Caliphate after the fall of Umayyad Caliphate. The dynasty descended from Muhammad's uncle, Al-Abbas ibn Abd al-Muttalib, from whom the dynasty takes its name (wikipedia).
